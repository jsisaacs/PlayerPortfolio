Frequently Asked Questions

What is Player Portfolio?

Player Portfolio is an open market that allows you to invest in a fantasy player’s season production by buying or selling “Shares” of that player. Player Portfolio is unique because it allows you to buy or sell any player at any timethroughout the season.

What Sports Are Available?

NFL, NHL, NBA, and MLBathletes will be availableto trade.

How Does It Work?

Player Portfolio is an open market,so it requires that a buyer agrees with a seller to trade a player at an agreed uponprice.The buyer then owns the right to that player’s “Share”. The seller receives the amount that the buyer was willing to pay for that “Share”.

What is the Value of a “Share” of a Player?

The value of a “Share” of a player is directly related to the amount of points that player scores in that season.The relation of fantasy points to real dollars varies by sport:

NFL: 100 Fantasy Points = $1.00 USD

NBA: 1,000 Fantasy Points = $1.00 USD

NHL: 100 Fantasy Points = $1.00 USD

MLB: 1,000 Fantasy Points = $1.00 USD

How Are Shares Generated?

Shares are generated when a new buyer and a new seller agree to trade a player. If either the buyer or seller is trading previously generated shares, then those shares are just changing hands, not new shares being generated.

How Can I Sell Shares Without Already Owning Them?

This is often referred to in the investing world as “Short Selling” or borrowing money to sell the sharesnowand then buying back the shares during or at the end of the season. The reason for this is if a player’s price is higher than you think it should be and you don’t already have shares of that player, you can “Short Sell” thatplayer which means you’re selling the shares at the current price but have to buy back the shareslater,at a price that you think will be cheaper.

How Much Money Can I Make?

Player Portfolio is speculative and is considered sports gambling. However, you can make a high return on your money if you pick the right players. The difference between the share value at the end of the season (or earlier if you wish to close your position to realize profits) and the price you paid for the share is your profit or loss. Buying underrated players or “selling short”overrated players can result in consistent profits. Player Portfolio takes the week-to-week randomness out of fantasy sports gambling.
